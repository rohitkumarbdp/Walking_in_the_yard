Theorem

Let \( M \) be an arbitrary set of \( n \) vectors in \( \mathbb{R}^d \) such that the length of each vector \( v \in M \) is at most 1 (in Euclidean terms), and the sum of all vectors in \( M \) equals 0. Then, it is possible to arrange all vectors of \( M \) into a sequence \((v_1, v_2, \ldots, v_n)\) such that the length of the sum of the first \( k \) vectors \((v_1 + v_2 + \ldots + v_k)\) is at most \( d \) for every \( k = 1, 2, \ldots, n\).
